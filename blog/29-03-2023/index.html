What is SAP?

By Lain Kopil

Short Answer: It is a Enterprise Resource Planning system (ERP)

Its kind of like a mission control center for orginizations

ERP software can manage things like

        -Finance 
            ~Budgetting
            ~Reports
            ~Forcasting 
        -Accounting
            ~Invoicing / Receipts
        -Human resources
            ~Hiring
            ~Talent (Having the right people in the right places): Identification, Learning and Development, Career management (aspirations), Aquisition
            ~Scheduling: Shifts, Sick days
            ~Productivity: Attendance, Software use tracking, Goal setting, Superviser feedback system, Training
            ~Payroll: Benefits information (Health insurance, retirement plans, employee perks), payment calculations, paycheck generation
            ~Communication: team management, inner social networking
            ~Team management: meeting plans, team building, employee satisfaction
            ~Project planning: task creation, task allocation
            ~Orginization processes documentation
        -Supply chain
            ~Procurement / Manufacturing
            ~Transportation
            ~Inventory management
            ~Vendor relations
        -Customer relationships (CRM)
            ~Social media tools: Online communitys, SM post automation, feed agregation
            ~Quality assurance
            ~Blogging tools
            ~Event planning
            ~Customer feedback
            ~Leads
            ~Reputation / Public perception: Data transperancy portal
            ~Automated customer support systems: Customer support web portal, common Q&A, support ticket request, telecommunication systems automation, email
            ~Human customer support systems
        -Business Intelligence (BI)
            ~Database: File syncronization, Migration, Centrallized, Encryption / Security
            ~Computational Resources
            ~Statistics & Analysis
            ~System administration: User account control, permissions, roles
            ~Document templates
            ~Software library
            ~Modular software systems (APIs, Integrations, Web apps)
        -Research and Development
            ~Testing
        ~Legal and compliance

Overall, an ERP system provides businesses with a comprehensive, integrated view of their operations, helping them to streamline their processes, reduce costs, manage orginization resources and improve overall efficiency.

Speculations on future ERP software solutions

Here are my top insights into where ERP will be heading in the near future

Cloud computing

ERP software has always had the centrallized database at the core of its implementations. But what happens if your datacenter is destroyed?
Many large tech orginizations worked on solving these problems through the 2010s resulting in enterprise products like Google Cloud Platform, Amazon EC2 Instances, Microsoft Azure Cloud, and Alibaba Cloud.
Procucts like these not only to make sure database information can't be lost from a single location, but significantly increase server load times on a global operations scale.
New technology wireless technologies like 5G are paving the way for armies of IOT sensors and also creating new ways for people to work remotly. ERP systems and remote computing should become accesible from a web browser instance whether its on your phone, tablet, low spec computer, or full workstation. Maybe we will also see some real use cases for blockchain (its a distrubuted database at its core) in creating more of a transperant ecosystem between orginizations. With akash cloud I believe we will also be seeing the democratization of computer resources and the cost of "renting/borrowing" things like storage and computational power will lessen the financial barriers for orginizations in need of highly computational solutions (Machine learning solutions etc)

Accoutability

Its becoming increasingly more important to be aware of the impact of our actions. Next gen ERP software will account for environmental and social impact of products and services. One example of this would be acounting for the sum of your orginizatoins carbon footprint through all its business procceses in addition to waste management. Another example could relate to anti corruption practices and orginization data transperancy.

Digital Twins

IMO this is going to be one of the coolest most hyped up new feature comming to ERP (Might have something to do with my video game addiction I had when I was younger) There are a lot of software companies talking about digital twin technology but I think we are still at the point of high expectations and have not gone through the motions to realizing the practical aspects of this technology. At its core this technology is about having realtime representations of systems informed by gathering data from a vast array of sensors (often explored as some kind of 3D visualization)

Odoo is a popular open source ERP software solution that is used by many businesses around the world. It offers modules for accounting, inventory management, human resources, CRM, and more. Odoo also has a strong community of developers and users who contribute to the development of the software.
